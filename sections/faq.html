Frequently Asked Questions

When is the deadline for filing annual tax returns in Nigeria?

For individuals, personal income tax returns are due by March 31st of each year. For companies, returns are due within 6 months of the company's year-end. We help you track these deadlines so you never miss them.

What documents do I need to register an LLC?

You'll need valid means of identification for all directors/shareholders, passport photographs, proof of address, and your proposed company name. We'll guide you through the exact requirements based on your specific situation.

How long does LLC registration take?

Typically, the CAC registration process takes 7-14 business days once all documents are submitted. Name reservation takes 1-2 days. We'll keep you updated at every stage.

What's the difference between an LLC and a Business Name?

An LLC (Limited Liability Company) is a separate legal entity that protects your personal assets from business liabilities. A Business Name is simply a trading name registered to you as an individual—your personal assets remain at risk. We recommend LLCs for most businesses.

Do I need to file taxes if my business made no profit?

Yes. In Nigeria, you must file tax returns regardless of profit. Filing "nil" returns keeps you compliant and avoids penalties. We can handle this for you quickly and affordably.

Can you help with tax clearance certificates?

Absolutely. We assist with obtaining Tax Clearance Certificates (TCC) which are required for government contracts, bank loans, and various business transactions.

What are the penalties for late tax filing?

Penalties vary but can include fines, interest on unpaid taxes (typically 21% per annum), and potential prosecution for persistent non-compliance. It's always better to file on time—we help ensure you do.

Yes! We serve clients across Nigeria. Most of our processes are handled digitally, so location is not a barrier. Simply reach out via WhatsApp or email to get started.
